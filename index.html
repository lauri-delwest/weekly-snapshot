The Week in Review · Leadership Summary

Biggest Takeaways

Stabilized occupancy rose 0.3 pp to 93.4% this week, driven by Holly38 absorbing 12 June move-ins and The Rowhomes jumping 7.5 pp to 95.5% on five move-ins and zero move-outs. High Line Pointe holds at 95.2% and projects to 96.8% by 6/30.

The leasing funnel strengthened. Tours attended rose to 36 (vs. a 31-week average) and applications started hit 25 (vs. an 18-week average, up 39%). Park Hill 4000 led all communities with 9 applications started and 4 approved this week, which is the deepest single-week pipeline in the portfolio and the clearest sign that its inventory is moving.

Two communities need immediate attention. Park Hill 4000 sits below 90% with 17 vacants and 9 notices, projecting flat at 89.8% through 6/30. The Landing holds at 93.7% today but its projection slides to 91.5% by 7/7 as 11 notices outpace 5 pre-leased move-ins. Both communities need backfill pipelines in motion before month-end.

The Vista executed its first 8 move-ins and projects 19.5% occupancy by 7/7 on a 13-unit July wave. With 9 tours attended and 4 applications started this week, the funnel is active. Converting undated applicants to firm move-in dates is the August pacing lever.

Net: a strong funnel week with occupancy ticking up. Park Hill 4000 and The Landing are the two near-term risks to manage. Below are the three biggest opportunities to act on this week.

Portfolio Focus This Week — Top 3 Opportunities
1

Watch Park Hill 4000 — and watch the pipeline. 89.8% occupancy, 17 vacants, 9 on notice. This week it ran the deepest funnel in the portfolio: 40 leads, 11 tours attended, 9 applications started, 4 approved. Those 4 approvals need to close and the July pre-leased wave needs to execute to reach the 91.6% projection.

2

Protect The Landing against a step-down. 11 notices on the board, 0 tours attended this week (4 were booked), and a projection sliding from 93.7% today to 91.5% by 7/7. Investigate the tour no-show rate, convert the 3 started applications, and build the July/August backfill pipeline before month-end.

3

Execute the Vista July wave. 13 move-ins are projected by 7/7, lifting occupancy from 6.8% to 19.5%. The funnel is active — 8 tours attended and 4 applications started this week. Converting undated applicants to firm dates is the August pacing lever.
